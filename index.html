The Experience

Welcome to The Polaris

Just 1.3 km from Fort Kochi Beach, The Polaris is a quiet, nature-inspired homestay set in a calm neighborhood close to cafés, art spaces, and heritage streets. Designed with hints of Kerala architecture and clean modern interiors, it offers comfortable rooms, essential amenities, and a relaxed atmosphere—perfect for unwinding after a day of exploring.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you allow early check-ins or late check-outs? expand_more

We do our best to accommodate these requests based on availability. Please let us know your arrival time in advance.

Is food available at the homestay? expand_more

We don't run a full restaurant, but breakfast is included. You are also surrounded by spectacular cafes and seafood spots in Fort Kochi, all within a short 5-minute walk.

Can you help arrange local tours? expand_more

Absolutely! We gladly assist in booking reliable airport transfers, scenic backwater tours, and Kathakali show tickets through our trusted local partners.

Is parking available at the property? expand_more

Yes, we offer safe, complimentary parking for our guests on the premises. Just let us know you're driving so we can reserve a spot.

Is there Wi-Fi and is it suitable for remote work? expand_more

Yes! We provide high-speed, complimentary Wi-Fi across the entire property. Many guests love working from our shaded verandas or the quiet courtyard.

The Best of Kochi, On Foot

Beyond our doorstep lies a city woven from centuries of stories. Here are the hand-picked essentials that define the coastal soul of Fort Kochi.

Heritage Icons

The Chinese Fishing Nets

Witness the rhythmic dance of the giant cantilevered nets against the setting sun at the Kochi beachfront.

Find your way arrow_forward
Architecture

Santa Cruz Basilica

Step into one of India’s most elegant cathedrals, known for its Gothic-inspired interiors and historic grandeur.

Just 500m away arrow_forward
Cafe Culture

Loafing on Princess Street

Fort Kochi’s oldest street is lined with European-colonial houses hosting some of the city's finest artisan cafes.

Walk the streets arrow_forward
Royal History

The Dutch Palace

A colonial-era palace gifted by the Portuguese. It houses some of the most stunning mythological murals in India and offers a window into Kochi's royal past.

Walk through history arrow_forward
First Church

St. Francis Church

The oldest European church in India. It's famous for being the original burial site of Vasco da Gama and stands as a quiet, powerful piece of colonial heritage.
